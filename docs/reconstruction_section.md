# Lambda Reconstruction — Paper Section Draft

$\Lambda^0$ and $\bar{\Lambda}^0$ hyperons were reconstructed via their dominant weak decay channels $\Lambda^0 \rightarrow p + \pi^{-}$ and $\bar{\Lambda}^0 \rightarrow \bar{p} + \pi^{+}$, respectively, with a branching ratio of 63.9\%. Reconstruction was performed using the KFParticle package~\cite{Zyzak2016}, a Kalman filter-based vertex fitting framework that simultaneously fits the decay vertex, propagates full covariance matrices through the detector magnetic field, and assigns PDG mass hypotheses to daughter tracks. Daughter tracks were required to have transverse momentum $0.15 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/$c$ and a three-dimensional distance of closest approach to the primary vertex of less than 3.0 cm. For each $\Lambda$ candidate, the two daughter tracks were fitted to a common secondary vertex; candidates were retained if the three-dimensional decay length exceeded 5.0 cm, the decay length significance $L/\sigma_L > 10$, and the $\chi^2/\mathrm{ndf}$ of the Kalman vertex fit was less than 10. These topological selections suppress the combinatorial background while preserving a high signal efficiency.

The invariant mass distribution of $p\pi^-$ ($\bar{p}\pi^+$) pairs was computed from the KFParticle-fitted four-momenta. In each centrality and rapidity bin, the distribution was fitted using an extended binned negative log-likelihood method (iminuit) with a double-Gaussian signal and a second-order polynomial background, both defined within a truncated range of $1.0$--$1.2$ GeV/$c^2$. A single Gaussian was used as a fallback when the two Gaussian components exhibited a width ratio exceeding a factor of five. The fit yields the signal $S(m)$ and background $B(m)$ distributions as continuous functions of invariant mass.

The directed flow signal $v_1^\mathrm{sig}$ was extracted via a simultaneous fit to the invariant-mass dependence of the raw flow observable $\langle\cos(\phi - \Psi_\mathrm{EPD})\rangle$, modeled as:
$$
v_1(m) = \frac{B(m)}{S(m)+B(m)}\,v_1^\mathrm{bkg}(m) \;+\; \frac{S(m)}{S(m)+B(m)}\,v_1^\mathrm{sig},
$$
where $v_1^\mathrm{bkg}(m) = p_0 + p_1(m - m_\Lambda)$ is assumed to vary linearly with invariant mass, and $v_1^\mathrm{sig}$ is a single constant representing the true hyperon signal. The fit proceeded in two stages: the background flow parameters $p_0$ and $p_1$ were first constrained using only sideband bins (outside $3\sigma$ of the signal peak), then fixed while $v_1^\mathrm{sig}$ was extracted from a fit over all mass bins. Minimization used a robust least-squares cost function (soft-$\ell_1$ loss) via Minuit.

The first-order event plane $\Psi_1$ was reconstructed from the East and West segments of the Event Plane Detector (EPD), which accepts particles at pseudorapidities well separated from midrapidity TPC acceptance by at least one unit in $\eta$, with larger gaps at higher collision energies, effectively suppressing autocorrelations. The event plane resolution was estimated using the sub-event method with the East and West EPD halves, and corrections were applied per centrality class for each collision energy.

Systematic uncertainties were evaluated by varying the analysis conditions across several independent sources: the primary vertex $z$-position selection (sys tag 1), the minimum number of TPC hit points required for daughter tracks (sys tag 2), and the KFParticle topological cut parameters — specifically tightening the maximum distance between daughter tracks to 0.8 cm and the decay length requirement to 3.0 cm (sys tag 3). Additional systematic variations included the rapidity range used in the $dv_1/dy$ slope fit (sys tag 5) and the polynomial order of the $v_1^\mathrm{bkg}(m)$ parameterization (sys tag 6). The total systematic uncertainty was taken as the quadrature sum of significant deviations from the default result, normalized by a divisor of $\sqrt{3}$ consistent with a uniform distribution assumption.
